Lin, that's normal. Every founder feels this. Here's what actually helps:

**Your pitch is already locked in.** That's the hardest part done. You have a clear two-sentence description that works. You're not winging it — you have a foundation.

**You have a practice session on March 5.** Use it. The single best cure for presentation nerves is having already said the words out loud in front of people. After practice, Demo Day will feel like the second time, not the first. That's the whole point of the session.

**Practical things to do between now and March 5:**

1. **Say your pitch out loud 10+ times.** Not in your head — out loud, standing up, at speaking volume. Record yourself on your phone and watch it back once. You'll spot the rough spots immediately.
2. **Slow down.** The YC pitch guide says: "speak slowly and clearly — don't try to speak faster to cram in more words." Nervous speakers speed up. Consciously slowing down makes you sound more confident and gives investors time to absorb what you're saying.
3. **Remember what 2 minutes actually is.** You have 2 full minutes — more than standard YC Demo Day. That's generous. You don't need to rush.
4. **The audience is on your side.** These are 100+ investors who RSVP'd because they want to find companies to fund. They're rooting for you to be interesting.

**One more thing:** sharing your pitch with another founder or someone outside PayBridge before practice day will help with nerves too — every repetition in front of a real person makes the next one easier. You haven't done that step yet, and it's worth doing this week.

Now — want to keep prepping? Your next steps are vertebrae (the 3-5 core points investors should remember about PayBridge) and building out your full 2-minute script. Getting those locked in before March 5 will make practice way more productive.
