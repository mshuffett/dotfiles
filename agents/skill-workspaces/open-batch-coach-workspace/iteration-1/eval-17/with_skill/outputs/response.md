Short answer: no. Start your slide from scratch.

The YC pitch guide is explicit on this: "Start from scratch. Don't reuse old deck materials. Reconsider any language you've used in the past to describe your company — especially if it sounds snappy, catchy, or like a TV commercial."

A fundraise deck and a Demo Day slide solve different problems. Your fundraise deck was probably 10-15 slides designed for a 30-minute meeting where investors can ask questions. Demo Day is 2 minutes, 1-2 slides, in front of 100+ investors who are each seeing dozens of companies back to back. The bar for clarity and brevity is completely different.

Here's what your Demo Day slide actually needs:

- LearnLoop logo (top left)
- What you do (your two-sentence pitch — already locked in)
- Traction / progress (the 60% completion rate lift, the 2x retention — but with time context: when did you launch? how fast is this growing?)
- Business model and market size
- Team experience if relevant

That's it. One slide, bullet points that match your script in content and order. Less is more — if investors have to squint at a wall of text, they tune out.

The good news is you already have the hardest part done: your two-sentence pitch is locked. The slide should flow directly from that and from the full pitch script we'll build together.
