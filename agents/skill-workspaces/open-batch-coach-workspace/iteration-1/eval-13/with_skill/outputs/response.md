Short answer: probably not, and here's why.

The pitch guide FAQ addresses this directly — if you're showing off an app or website, you don't need to. At a glance, most apps and websites look the same, and a nice UI screenshot doesn't move the needle with investors. What moves the needle is what FormStack *does*: turning 3-week paper processes into 2-day digital ones. Your numbers already tell that story better than a screenshot can.

That said, you do have an option: the format allows a second slide with a demo video uploaded to YouTube, as long as your total presentation stays under 2 minutes. So if you have a compelling before/after walkthrough — say, showing a government clerk going from a stack of paper to an approved application in minutes — that could work. But it eats into your 2 minutes, and you'd need to weigh whether that time is better spent on traction, market size, or your closing.

My recommendation: skip the demo on the slide. Use your one slide for the essentials (logo, what you do, traction, business model/market, team if relevant) and keep the bullet points matched to your script. Your locked pitch already paints a clear picture — "50,000 forms for 12 state agencies" is more memorable than any UI walkthrough.

If you feel strongly about it, we can talk through what a demo video would look like and whether it earns its time. But I'd prioritize getting your vertebrae and full pitch script locked first — those will tell you whether a demo adds anything or just takes time away from stronger material.
