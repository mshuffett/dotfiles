# Positioning & Pitch Copy (Michael's voice)

Michael has a documented **AI-slop allergy** (it's flagged in his own gap-analysis notes). His own plain lines routinely beat generated alternatives. When a session ignored this and sprayed 13 tagged buzzword one-liners, he called them "pretty terrible... ai slop" and picked his own line instead: *"we're building a network of increasingly autonomous companies."*

## React, don't generate — his voice is the asset

- When Michael gives you draft lines, **sharpen HIS lines** — don't replace them with a fresh list of your own. Default to reacting, not generating.
- If he asks for "options" or "directions," give **3–4 max**, each built *from his phrasing*, not a spray of 10+ clever alternatives. A long tagged list reads as slop and buries the good one.
- Calibrate to his voice on the **first pass**. Don't generate slop and then apologize — that wastes a turn. Assume the anti-slop bar from the start.
- When unsure, **ask which mechanic or word is doing the work** ("is it the LinkedIn twist, the YC twist, or the plain 'runs itself'?") rather than guessing with more volume.

## The mechanic that makes his lines land

**Plain, spoken words + one concrete, slightly surprising twist.**

- "it's like LinkedIn, but the profiles are companies — and the companies run themselves"
- "...except it runs itself" / "companies that increasingly run themselves"

The familiar anchor (LinkedIn, YC) + the twist ("...except it runs itself") is what works. Concrete image over abstract category.

## Anti-slop blocklist — startup mad-libs to avoid

Do NOT reach for these unless Michael uses them first:

- Abstract category nouns stacked as the product: "the **layer**," "a **primitive**," "the **engine**," "the **infrastructure**," "the **operating system** for X."
- "**AWS for** X," "**LinkedIn for** the Y era," "**the platform for** Z" as filler analogies (the analogy only works when it's genuinely apt and concrete, like his own LinkedIn twist — not as a template).
- Buzzword chains ("memory, agents, network, and capital in one layer").

## Where a table IS the right move

The anti-slop rule is about *voice/copy*, not analysis. For a **strategic question** ("what are the business-model options?"), a crisp comparison table (model / who pays / how it scales / the catch) works well and he engages with it. Reserve the spray-of-options instinct for structured analysis, never for the copy itself.
