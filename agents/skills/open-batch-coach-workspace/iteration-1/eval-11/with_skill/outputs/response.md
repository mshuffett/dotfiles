Great question. Vertebrae are the 3-5 core points you most want investors to remember about StackShip after your pitch. Think of them as the backbone of your entire Demo Day presentation -- the key facts and arguments that make someone afraid *not* to invest.

Looking at the pitch guide, your vertebrae should answer questions like:

- What are you building and for whom?
- Why hasn't this been done before?
- Why is it hard?
- Why is this an opportunity not to be missed?

They're not just for Demo Day either. You'll use them in investor conversations, your intro blurb (the paragraph investors read when deciding whether to take a meeting), and follow-up emails after Demo Day.

Here's what good vertebrae look like, using PostHog as an example:

1. What they do: "PostHog is open source product analytics -- like Mixpanel, but open source."
2. Traction: "Our HN launch was the all-time most upvoted B2B software launch. After 2 weeks: 400 deployments, 1,500 GitHub stars, growing ~20/day."
3. Why this approach wins: "Going after developers via open source means zero cost, zero info-sec process. Being on-premise means we can scale to data-sensitive enterprises."
4. Team: "James sold $5M ARR deals. Tim was the fastest developer out of 50 at the company they spent 4 years at."
5. Market: "There are 8-10x more developers than product people. We can bring data-driven decisions to every company."

Notice how each point is specific and memorable -- not generic things like "large market opportunity."
