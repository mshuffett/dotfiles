$500M is on the small side for a venture pitch. The YC guide's FAQ is direct about this: if your market is under $1B, consider whether you can expand the scope.

But before you decide to leave it out, let's think about this bottom-up for PetRx. A few questions:

1. **How are you calculating $500M?** Is that just the US pet pharmacy market? Are you including only the online segment, or all pet medication sales?
2. **Are there adjacent segments you'd naturally expand into?** Pet supplements, pet food, vet telehealth, pet insurance — does your distribution model (same-day delivery + pharmacy infrastructure) open any of those doors?
3. **Is pet ownership or pet spending growing fast?** A $500M market growing 15-20% annually tells a different story than a flat one.

If you can credibly frame a larger addressable market — say, total pet health spending that you'd capture a slice of over time — then include it with the bottom-up math. The Airbnb example from the guide is instructive: they didn't say "the short-term rental market." They said "1 billion hotel nights per year x $20 per night = $20B." Start from the number of pet owners, prescriptions per year, and your revenue per prescription.

If you genuinely can't get the number above $1B with honest math, it's better to leave market size out entirely and use that time on something stronger — like your traction (10,000 prescriptions in 6 months is compelling) or your growth rate. A small market mentioned is worse than no market mentioned, because it gives investors a reason to pass.
