# The Inner Stop-Gap — On Self-Doubt as a Structural Force

## Summary
A voice-captured insight: the inner voice that says "I'm not good enough" isn't
random — it's functioning as a brake, a structural stop-gap against some feared
chaos. The key move is treating it as separable, something that can be factored
out rather than managed or suppressed.

## Key Details
The framing is precise: this isn't "I feel inadequate sometimes." It's a claim
that the inner critic has a *functional role* — it believes it's doing something
useful (preventing chaos). That's why it's sticky. Naming the function is the
first step to seeing it as a structure, not as truth.

"It can be factored out" is the insight. Engineering metaphor applied to
psychology: if it's a factor, it can be isolated and removed without destroying
the underlying thing.

## Source
- Todoist item: "There's like this force in all of us that says I'm not good
  enough. It's like a stop gap to keep the world from chaos. But it can be
  factored out."

## Connections
- Related concepts: IFS (Internal Family Systems), parts work, self-compassion
- The "protective function" framing is core to IFS — the inner critic as a
  protector running an outdated program
