What happened — root cause

A large Claude Code workflow fan-out (wf_2a62ba19, 104 subagent processes) exhausted all 16 GB of RAM. The box had zero swap, so instead of cleanly OOM-killing one process, the kernel fell into an unbounded memory-reclaim livelock — thrashing the page cache, starving every process of CPU. It went silent at 19:15 PT and AWS marked it impaired (OS hung; hardware & disk stayed fine) for ~2 h until a hard reboot.
Smoking gun: all periodic logging flatlines at 01:22:05 UTC, then only "Under memory pressure, flushing caches" until journald dies at 02:15:53 UTC — and zero Out of memory: Killed process lines anywhere (a livelock, not a clean OOM kill). Two amplifiers: no swap and no SSM role (so no out-of-band shell to rescue it — turning a stall into a 2-hour outage). Full RCA: scratch/rootcause/ROOT-CAUSE.md.
