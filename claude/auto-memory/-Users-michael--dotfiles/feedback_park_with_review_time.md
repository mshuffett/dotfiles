Michael (Jun 2026, verbatim): "when we move stuff we need to make sure we have clear times to review it," and (prior) "we need those tasks to be on dates basically or they might not get resurfaced."

Never park / move a task or idea into a backlog he doesn't review. Every parked item gets EITHER:

- a **concrete date** so it resurfaces in Todoist (date = commitment — see the coach Due-Date Rule: don't date what you wouldn't bet $20 on doing; but if it's parked-not-dated it MUST have a reviewed home instead), OR
- a **home that is explicitly walked during the Saturday weekly review** (the GTD trigger-list scan + inbox-triage phases of the coach weekly-review SOP).

If using a backlog bucket, **confirm it is actually in the weekly-review scan before routing there**; otherwise prefer dating. Watch the anti-pattern: "Someday/Maybe" had ~150 items and was never checked — parking there = silently losing the item.

**Why:** a parked item with no date and no reviewed home disappears; that breaks trust in the system and in me. A review time is exactly what makes "parked" different from "dropped."
**How to apply:** when filing / parking / triaging any Todoist or Obsidian item, or routing inbox to someday/backlog — attach a date OR a weekly-review-walked home, and state which. Pairs with [[system-change-visibility]] (the review time is part of the per-item plan he signs off on); extends the coach Due-Date Rule.
