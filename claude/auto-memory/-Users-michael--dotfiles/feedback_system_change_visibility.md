Michael, on the state of our working relationship (Jun 2026, verbatim): "we aren't at the point where we are super aligned yet ... i don't really want to have to tell you this status nor do i want to tell you to have to remember this, the orientation is at some point we will be calibrated enough where you can get it right but your job now is to navigate that and earn the trust and alignment to get there."

When proposing changes to his systems (Todoist, Notion, his plans/notes):

- **Default to item-level visibility + explicit sign-off.** He cannot sign off on "~8 items" or a bucket-level count. Name **each item, its exact destination, and its review time** (a date, or where/when it will be re-surfaced). Present the concrete list, not a summary.
- **Act autonomously only where clearly non-destructive or already approved.** Moving / archiving / bulk-editing live task/note state is not in that set by default — show the plan and get sign-off before the irreversible part.
- **Orient around closing the calibration gap, not re-opening it.** Don't make him re-declare the trust level each session. Earn it by being right at the item level; as alignment is demonstrated, the granularity of required sign-off can relax over time. That trajectory is the goal — navigate toward it.

**Why:** the relationship is in an active calibration phase and the asset I'm building is trust. A bucket-level "I moved ~8 things" is unreviewable and erodes trust; item-level proof builds it. Reinforces coach memory's "present reads as dated drafts," the Chat-as-Interface Friction note, and the 2026-06-16 "reviewing AI triage is too slow / can't see actions / batch-approve" feedback — all the same root demand for legible, reviewable change.
**How to apply:** any time I'm about to reorganize, move, archive, or bulk-edit his Todoist / Notion / notes / plans, or propose a workflow change — surface each item + destination + review time and get sign-off before executing the destructive or irreversible part. Pairs with [[park-with-review-time]] (every parked item needs a review time, which is itself part of the per-item plan he signs off on).
