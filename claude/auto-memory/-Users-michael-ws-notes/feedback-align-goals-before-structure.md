When asked to Socratically question Michael before writing a doc, I led with "how should the doc be organized?" He rejected it: "I don't know how to answer that… rather than focusing on structure we should focus on aligning on the goals and methods… the structure kind of falls out from the goals."

**Why:** Structure is downstream of goals. Asking for structure first is a false choice he can't answer and inverts the dependency order.

**How to apply:** For planning/writing tasks, run goal + method alignment first (what we're trying to achieve, constraints, principles, sequencing), then let information architecture emerge and confirm structure last. Related: when capturing, write only what's known/stated — don't fabricate; surface unknowns as explicit questions (his instruction: "write what you know… you shouldn't write what you don't know but write what I have said and any important questions").
