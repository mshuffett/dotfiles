After the Stage-3 deck exploration round (2026-07-01), Michael's verdict: "decent start" but it "jumped a bit too far into a limited number of specific copy or images" and "wasn't clear enough at the concept level — didn't feel MECE enough."

**Why:** Specific artifacts (headlines, generated art) presented before concept alignment force him to reverse-engineer the concept space from samples, and reject wording when the real problem is unagreed intent (e.g., the PBC slide: "I'm not even clear we're aligned on what we're trying to say here").

**How to apply:**
1. For each open item, first present a MECE map of *what could be said/shown* (the distinct concepts), get a gate on that.
2. After concept: **visual descriptions (text) before actual visuals** when iterating synchronously — whatever is fastest for iteration. Async/background work may generate real visuals, but never present ONE for yes/no: present spreads that narrow the latent space optimally — **Michael's time is the bottleneck**; each reaction should eliminate a family, not a sample.
3. Produce MORE volume than feels necessary; proactive visual directions for load-bearing choices.
4. Consider audience splits as a concept axis (e.g., regular-VC vs mission-aligned covers).
5. Voice-sensitive copy → context-inheriting fork agents or carry his verbatim quotes; cold subagents produce off-voice copy.
6. Fable forks: 1–2 max, highest-level (planning/concept) work only; opus for creative generation, sonnet for mechanical.
7. Self-reflect after each round and update the method log (deck doc "Process feedback" section).
